The "Golden Age Console" by Victor Talking Machine Company is a luxurious Victrola from the 1920s, designed as a stunning piece of furniture as well as a high-quality music player. The console cabinet is made from exquisite walnut wood with gold-plated accents and ornate detailing. Inside, it houses a powerful motor and turntable for playing shellac records, along with built-in storage for record collections. The sound is channeled through the cabinet, producing a warm, full-bodied audio experience. This Victrola is ideal for those who appreciate the grandeur and sophistication of early 20th-century design combined with superior audio performance.
